Questions

The honest answers.

Is Relay free? +

Yes. Joining is free — you earn credits by teaching and spend them to learn. No cash changes hands between peers.

What if I only want to learn? +

New members start with a few free credits to get going. But Relay works best when everyone shares something.

Is the teacher any good? +

Everyone is a verified student. You rate each session afterward — the best teachers rise to the top.

Who can join right now? +

We're starting with NYU students this fall. Add your email to be in the first cohort.

What about YouTube or AI? Why pay for skills? +

Tutorials can't look at your code in real time, correct your guitar fingering, or tell you your squat form is off. Relay is about live, personalized feedback from a real person — something AI and video can't replace.

What stops me from hoarding 100 credits? +

Nothing — but credits only have value when you spend them. If everyone hoards, no one learns. Credits expire after 6 months of inactivity to keep the system flowing.

What if the teacher doesn't show up? +

You get your credit back immediately. Teachers with repeated no-shows lose their ability to offer skills until they complete a session. Reviews are public, so good teachers rise.

How is a 30-min guitar lesson worth the same as Python? +

Time is the currency. A beginner teaching basic chords and an advanced coder debugging your script both give 30 minutes of focused attention. Skill level shows in the teacher's proficiency rating — pick someone whose level matches what you need.

Does Relay cost money? +

Joining is free. Credit exchanges between peers are free. For heavy users, a $4.99/month membership unlocks unlimited sessions and priority scheduling. Your first 3 credits are always on us.
