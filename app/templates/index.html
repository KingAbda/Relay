Honest answers

The questions students ask first.

1Is Relay free?

Joining is free. Students trade credits for sessions, so no cash changes hands between peers.

2What if I only want to learn?

You can start with free credits, then either teach to earn more or use a paid plan if you want predictable monthly credits.

3How do credits work?

Teach one 30-minute session to earn one credit. Spend one credit to book a 30-minute session with another student.

4What can I teach?

Anything useful to another student: debugging, resume feedback, Canva flyers, lifting form, budgeting, languages, music, or class prep.

5How do I know the teacher is good?

Profiles show completed sessions, reviews, and what the student says they can teach. No-shows and bad sessions hurt trust quickly.

6Why not YouTube or AI?

Relay is for live feedback: someone watching your form, debugging your actual code, reacting to your draft, or practicing with you in real time.

7Who can join right now?

The redesign is focused on the NYU pilot. Students can join the first cohort with a .edu email.

8What happens if someone flakes?

You get your credit back. Repeated no-shows lose trust and make it harder to keep offering sessions.
