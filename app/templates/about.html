About Relay

Trading skills, not money.

A peer-to-peer skill exchange built by students, for students — so learning something new never depends on what you can afford.

The idea

A structured way to trade what you know

No cash, just time. Thirty minutes of teaching earns one credit; spend credits to learn anything on the platform. Everyone is verified through their .edu email.

The data

The demand is already there

Across 300+ student interviews: 70% have a skill they'd want compensation for, 85% want to learn but can't always afford it, and 75% showed a supply-and-demand match.

The mission

Turn campus favor culture into fair culture

Every student has something to teach and something to learn. Relay connects them directly — without the awkwardness of putting a price on your knowledge.

The vision

Start at NYU, expand campus by campus

A world where any student can access any skill they want to learn — paid forward through teaching, not paid for in cash.

AT

Abdurrahman Touray — Founder

First-generation Gambian American, NYU 2026 (HEOP Scholar). Relay started in Abu Dhabi — a friend taught me music production, and I helped him start going to the gym. No money changed hands. That moment sparked a question: what if there were a system for this?

After 300+ student interviews and a 50-person pilot, Relay is built for students who want to learn without breaking the bank and teach without being undervalued.
