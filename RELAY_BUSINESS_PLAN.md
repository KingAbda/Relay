## 1. Executive Summary

**Relay** is a peer-to-peer **skill exchange** platform where students trade 30-minute teaching sessions using time-based credits. No cash changes hands between peers — you earn by teaching, spend by learning.

| Metric | Status |
|--------|--------|
| **Student Interviews** | 300+ completed |
| **Financial Status** | Break-even |
| **MVP** | Live and operational |
| **Target Audience** | NYU students (~60,000) |
| **Launch Window** | August 2026 (back-to-school) |
| **Monetization** | Freemium: Free (2 credits) → Starter ($14.99/mo) → Pro ($29.99/mo) |

### Mission
> *"The gym membership for your skills. Teach anything for 30 minutes, earn a credit, spend it to learn anything else."*

### Current Status
Relay is NOT a pre-revenue concept. It's a **live, break-even operation** with real users (300+ interviews, validated demand). The MVP is shipping. The challenge is **monetization, distribution, and quality control** — not product-market fit.

## 2. Why the Idea Works

### 2.1 Validated Demand
300+ student interviews confirmed:
- **70%** have a skill they'd teach if compensated fairly
- **85%** want to learn something but can't find/cost-justify a mentor
- **75%** supply-demand match rate — there's a teach for every learn
- Students spend **$40-80/hr** on tutoring, **$20/mo** on Skillshare — but crave *live, personalized* interaction

### 2.2 Network Effects
- **Teach → earn credits → learn → want more credits → teach more** — the loop feeds itself
- Each new user adds both supply (skills to teach) AND demand (skills to learn)
- NYU is a captive, dense audience — word spreads through dorms, clubs, group chats

### 2.3 Timing
| Factor | Why Now |
|--------|---------|
| **AI tutoring surge** | ChatGPT/Claude are great for answers, terrible for hands-on skills (guitar, lifting, public speaking) — *Relay fills the gap* |
| **Skill economy rising** | LinkedIn, Coursera, bootcamps all pushing skills-based hiring over degrees |
| **Students are broke** | Inflation, student loans — barter economy is more attractive than ever |
| **Post-COVID** | Students crave in-person, real-human interaction after years of screens |

### 2.4 Founder-Market Fit
You're not a consultant reading about student problems in a report. You are:
- An NYU student who **lives the pain** daily
- **Bootstrapped to break-even** with no outside funding
- **Talked to 300+ students** — deeper customer knowledge than any competitor
- **Built the MVP yourself** — technical execution + founder vision = rare combo

## 3. The 5 Risks (Addressed)

### Risk #1: Monetization — "Will students actually pay?"

| The Concern | The Reality |
|-------------|-------------|
| Students are broke and expect free | Private tutors cost **$40-80/hr**. A $14.99/mo plan is **1-2 UberEats deliveries**. |
| Freemium kills paid conversion | Free tier limits to 2 credits + 2 listings. Students hit the paywall FAST. |
| Competitors are free (YouTube, Discord) | YouTube can't correct your guitar fingering. Discord can't tell you your squat form is off. **Live > video.** |

**Verdict:** You don't need everyone to pay. If **15%** of 3,000 users convert to Starter ($14.99) = **$6,750/mo**. That's a business.

### Risk #2: Marketplace Graveyards — "What if nobody uses it?"

**Why Relay avoids this trap:**
- Every new user is both supply (teacher) AND demand (learner) — balanced growth is built in
- **300+ interviews = pre-existing demand.** You're not guessing.
- NYU is a captive audience (60K students in a dense campus)
- **Semester cadence** creates natural loops: "I learned Python last semester, now I can teach it"

### Risk #3: Quality Control — "What if teachers are bad?"

**Mitigation stack:**
1. **Rating system** — both sides rate after each session (already built)
2. **Proficiency dots** — skill level shown upfront (already built)
3. **No-show protection** — credit refunded, repeat offenders lose teaching privileges
4. **.edu verification** — baseline trust

### Risk #4: Vitamin vs Painkiller — "Would students miss Relay?"

**Right now:** Vitamin. Students would go back to "ask a friend" or YouTube.

**How to become a painkiller:**
- Embed Relay into **syllabuses** (professor says "use Relay for peer tutoring" = mandatory)
- Become the **official** peer learning tool for NYU clubs and student orgs
- Build **streak/reputation** system — "I'm a level 5 Python teacher" with social proof

### Risk #5: Competition

| Competitor | Relay's Advantage |
|------------|------------------|
| **YouTube tutorials** | Live, personalized feedback. Can't correct YOUR specific issue. |
| **Discord study servers** | Structured, time-boxed sessions with scheduling. No ghosting. |
| **AI tutoring (ChatGPT, Claude, Khanmigo)** | Great for academic Q&A. Useless for hands-on skills (gym, guitar, cooking, public speaking). |
| **Paid tutoring (Wyzant, Varsity Tutors)** | 10x cheaper. $14.99/mo vs $40-80/hr. |
| **Skillshare/Coursera** | Pre-recorded, not live. No interaction. No peer accountability. |

**Moat:** Live, peer-to-peer, .edu-verified, credit-based (not cash). The barter mechanic keeps prices at zero while creating genuine value.

## 4. Monetization Strategy

### Pricing Tiers

| Tier | Price | Credits/Month | Active Listings | Key Perks |
|------|-------|---------------|-----------------|-----------|
| **Free** | **$0** | 2 (signup bonus) | 2 | Browse, earn-by-teaching |
| **Starter** | **$14.99/mo** | 5 monthly | 3 | Verified badge, priority matching |
| **Pro** | **$29.99/mo** | Unlimited | Unlimited | Analytics, all perks |
| **Annual Pro** | **$249/yr** ($20.75/mo) | Unlimited | Unlimited | Save 31% vs monthly |

### Why These Prices Work
- **$5 was too cheap.** It signaled low quality and left money on the table.
- **$14.99** is less than one UberEats meal. Students spend this without thinking.
- **$29.99** is still 2-5x cheaper than a single private tutoring session.
- **Free tier hooks them** — after they invest time building their profile, switching cost is real.

### Credit Economy
| Action | Credit Impact |
|--------|--------------|
| Sign up (free) | +2 credits |
| Teach a 30-min session | +1 credit |
| Learn a 30-min session | -1 credit |
| Refer a friend | +1 credit (both sides) |
| Starter plan renewal | +5 credits monthly |
| Pro plan | Unlimited sessions |

## 10. Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Probability | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|------------|----------|------------|
| Students won't pay | MEDIUM | HIGH | Freemium model + low starting price + survey validation before building |
| Quality drops at scale | HIGH | HIGH | Rating system P0 + no-show penalties + .edu verification |
| Competition copies | MEDIUM | MEDIUM | Execute faster + build community (network effects are a moat) |
| Burnout | HIGH | HIGH | Keep scope small. One university. One vertical. One pricing lever. |
| Low repeat usage | MEDIUM | HIGH | Referral rewards, streak system, syllabus integration |

### Contingency Plans
- **If 0% pay in month 1:** Pivot to donation model + campus partnerships
- **If signups stall:** Go all-in on one channel (professor referral or club partnerships)
- **If competition launches:** Double down on what they can't copy — your community, your interviews, your relationships

## Appendix A: Emergency Playbook

**If a launch goes wrong (zero traction):**
1. Don't panic. You're break-even = you can't lose money.
2. Pick ONE channel and go deep (professors > everything else)
3. Talk to 10 users who signed up but didn't come back. Find the pattern.
4. Fix the #1 complaint. Relaunch the next week.
5. If after 3 attempts nothing moves, consider pausing. Not failing — pausing.

**Your safety net:** You're a NYU student with technical skills, an audience, and multiple income streams. Relay is one bet. Don't bet the farm.
