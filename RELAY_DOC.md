## The Core Idea

Relay is a **peer-to-peer skill exchange platform** for university students. Instead of paying cash, students trade skills using a time-based credit system.

**30 minutes of teaching = 1 credit**  
Spend credits to learn anything on the platform. No money changes hands between peers.

---

## Origin Story

Spring 2025 — Abda was at NYU Abu Dhabi. A friend taught him music production; in exchange, Abda helped him start going to the gym. No money, just skill trade. That moment sparked the question: *What if there was a structured system for this?*

After 15+ conversations abroad, the pattern was clear: people have skills, people want skills, but there's no trusted way to exchange them.

Fall 2025 — Social Entrepreneurship class at NYU with **Daniel Porter** (founder of Overtime). The assignment: build a real business. Abda's team conducted **300 student interviews** across NYU and NYU Abu Dhabi to validate the problem.

## How It Works

### The Relay Race (Metaphor)
1. **Earn by Teaching** — Share your skill for 30 minutes → earn 1 credit
2. **Spend by Learning** — Use credits to access skills from other students
3. **Verified Through .edu** — Campus email ensures trust and community boundaries

### Example Flow
- Student A teaches 2 voice lessons → earns 2 credits
- Student A spends credits on 2 design lessons for their portfolio site
- Student B (the designer) now has credits to learn something else

### User Journey
1. Sign up with `.edu` email
2. List what you can teach + what you want to learn *(both required)*
3. Browse available skills or get matched
4. Request a session
5. Teach → earn credits → learn → repeat

## Critical Feedback Received

### From Professor Porter
- **Strengths:** Cleanest presentation in class, Apple-event level delivery, confident Q&A, strong storytelling
- **Critiques:**
  - Profit margins looked closer to gross than net
  - What happens when a teacher is ineffective? (quality control)
  - Why were sports/hobbies removed? Does narrowing limit reach?
  - Need to A/B test willingness to pay
  - Simplify the user journey — "Bar Test" (can you describe it in one sentence in a bar?)

### From Peers (Class Feedback)
- Supply-demand imbalance — what if someone only wants to learn?
- Skill verification & quality control — how to ensure good teaching?
- Marketplace logistics — no-shows, repeat usage
- Pricing clarity — how do credits map to different skill levels?
- Will YouTube/AI free content undermine value?

### From Daniel Porter (In-Person)
- **"What's the single biggest challenge about a marketplace? Having enough buyers and enough matches."**
- **Advice:** Narrow scope. Launch with ONE vertical (like fitness or creative skills). Don't overcomplicate with personality matching.
- **Marketing:** Word of mouth has a viral coefficient k — it needs to be > 1 to sustain growth. Use channel sales (clubs, classes, organizations).
- **Attrition is massive** — 2.5% conversion was typical in mobile gaming.

## Key Strategic Questions (Open)

1. **Supply-Demand Flywheel** — How to ensure every learner also becomes a teacher? If credits drain without replenishment, the system dies. **(Current priority)**

2. **Vertical Narrowing** — Porter said launch with one vertical. Creative skills (45% teach) and Finance (40% demand) are the biggest pools. Which to pilot?

3. **Quality Control** — How to handle bad teachers, no-shows, and skill verification at scale?

4. **Credit Economics** — What prevents inflation/deflation of credits? What happens when someone accumulates 100 credits?

5. **Monetization Path** — Is the $5/mo membership the right model, or should it be pay-per-session cash option for pure consumers?

6. **Competition** — YouTube, Skillshare, Coursera, free tutoring centers. What's the moat?

## Appendix: The Meshr Concept (Earlier Iteration)

Before Relay, the idea was "Meshr" — a skill-bartering platform with personality compatibility matching (introvert/extrovert, learning styles). Porter advised against this:

> *"If you have 40 people who want to train people to work out, and 20 people who want piano lessons, you don't have a marketplace. And if you start adding personality gradations, you'll need thousands of people for everyone to find a match."*

**Lesson:** Start simple. Add filters later. Density first, personalization second.
